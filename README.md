# Teen Mental Health & Screen Time Analysis Dashboard

An interactive and data-driven dashboard analyzing the correlation between teenagers' digital habits, academic performance, and mental health indicators.

📊 **[Access the Interactive Dashboard Here](https://datastudio.google.com/reporting/b423c5d6-40b6-46ea-8648-fbde9616bb88/page/p_gwvus6et3d)**

---

## 📌 Project Overview
This project transforms raw data into an actionable storytelling dashboard using **Google Looker Studio**. The analysis focuses on understanding how deep teenagers are immersed in the digital world and how it potentially reflects on their school performance and psychological well-being (Stress, Anxiety, and Addiction levels).

The dataset covers a balanced sample of **1,200 students** with comprehensive metrics tracking daily social media hours, screen time before sleep, and clinical depression indicators.

---

## 🧠 Key Insights & Data Storytelling

### 📄 Page 1: Executive Summary
* **Digital Burden vs. Academics:** Teenagers in this dataset carry a heavy digital load, averaging **4.5 hours of social media daily**. Despite this intense digital activity, their academic performance remains stable at a moderate level (**2.99 out of a maximum scale of 4**). 
* **Stress Warning Sign:** The average stress level stands at **5.45 on a 1-10 scale**. Crossing the mid-point indicates that psychological pressure is starting to become a common reality for the majority of these teens.
* **Balanced Demographics:** The dataset holds a highly balanced gender distribution (**51.2% Male vs. 48.8% Female**), ensuring the analytical insights are unbiased and valid for both groups.
* **Platform Dominance:** **TikTok** and **Instagram** heavily dominate teenage attention (each approaching 400 active users), vastly outnumbering those who use both. Visual, short-form video content is clearly the most effective medium to deploy any youth mental health awareness campaigns.

### 📄 Page 2: Social Media Behavior
* **Age-Based Trends:** Digital screen time tends to peak during early-to-mid adolescence and experiences a slight decline toward late teenage years. This shift usually correlates with increased academic responsibilities or university preparations.
* **The Bedtime Routine Danger:** On average, teenagers spend **1.74 hours on screens right before sleeping**. This is a significant health concern; clinically, nearly 2 hours of blue light exposure right before bed disrupts melatonin production and severely lowers sleep quality.
* **Generational Habit, Not Gendered:** Digital behavior between genders is **almost identical**. Males average 4.56 hours on social media and 1.72 hours of bedtime screen time, while females average 4.51 hours on social media and 1.76 hours of bedtime screen time. Screen dependency is a generational phenomenon rather than a gender-specific issue.

### 📄 Page 3: Mental Health Overview
* **The Trio Mental Indicators:** When aligned, all three psychological indicators sit at a similar elevated level: **Stress (5.45)**, **Anxiety (5.64)**, and **Addiction (5.57)** on a 1-10 scale. This uniform elevation proves that digital addiction and mental pressure are highly interconnected.
* **Academic Resilience:** Despite facing moderate-to-high stress levels, both male and female students maintain a steady academic score near **3.0**. Gender does not make one group more academically vulnerable to mental pressure than the other.
* **The Window for Intervention:** An overwhelming majority (**97.4%**) of the teenagers currently fall into the `0` category (Not Clinically Depressed). This is the most critical takeaway: **Teens are currently stressed, but not yet clinically depressed.** This represents a vital window of opportunity for early educational and psychological intervention before conditions worsen.
